Buffon's Needle Simulation

This simulation visualizes Buffon’s Needle — a classic probability experiment that estimates the value of π through random motion. Needles of length L are dropped onto a surface with parallel lines spaced D units apart. By counting how many of the N total needles cross a line (C), we can estimate π using the formula: π ≈ (2 × L × N) / (D × C). Watch as the randomness converges toward mathematical truth — one needle at a time.
